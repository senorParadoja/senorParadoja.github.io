The Greatest and Best Song In The World

…a tribute

Still from dramatisation of events. From Left to Right: JB, KG.©2001 SME & Epic Records

A long time ago the members of Tenacious D — KG and JB — were hitchiking down a long and lonesome road whence shined a Shiney Demon in the middle of the road, who demanded them play the best song in the world. Lest he eat their souls.[1]

With no other choice than to accept the challenge, they each said “O.K.”. Tenacious D would play the first thing that came to their heads.[1] Which, as they sing in Tribute[1], a much later song chronicling the event:

Just so happened to be
The best song in the world.[1]

And also:

Look into my eyes
And it's easy to see
One and one make two
Two and one make three
It was destiny

Once every hundred-thousand years or so
When the sun doth shine
And the moon doth glow
And the grass doth grow[1]

Tenacious D recount how the beast [demon] was stunned upon hearing this song and questioned the duo if they were angels. To this the pair replied “Nay, we are but men”[1]. It is unclear whether RG and JB subsequently shouted “Rock” to the demon, or if this is simply a lyric their Tribute. What is clear is the demon's defeat — made explicit in the dramatisation of events.[2]

Depiction of demon being defeated from dramatisation of events.©2001 SME & Epic Records

Sadly, as Tenacious D's recount of events states, the greatest song in the world has been forgotten to time — and even they cannot recall the lyrics nor melody. Tenacous D make clear that the greatest song in the world “didn't even sound anything like”[1] the Tribute.
